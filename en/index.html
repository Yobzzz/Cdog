CDOG mining

1. What is mining?

To put it simply, mining is to deposit a pair of currencies of equal value to DeFis, such as DFS/EOS, CDOG/EOS, CDOG/USDT, etc.
For instance, if 1 EOS = 0.5 DFS now, then we need to deposit both 1 EOS and 0.5 DFS. Of course you can deposit more, but they have to be equal in value. Those who do mining are known as market merchants.



2. What is the benefits of mining?

To do mining that is to provided liquidity for decentralized exchanges. Users can exchange corresponding currencies in this liquidity pool. Therefore, market merchants as liquidity providers should enjoy the following benefits:

1.  Users need to pay a 0.3% handling fee for currency exchange. This part of the handling fee will be converted into DFS (a token) and distributed to the corresponding marketer, commonly known as market-making fee income.
2.  Beside participate in the mining of designated trading pairs, you can also participate in token mining. The greater the market-making capital, the more DFS obtained. 1+2 generally collectively referred to as mining.
3.  Participate in the mining of designated trading pairs. That is to say, the corresponding market-making LP (it can be simply understood as going to the bank to deposit money, and you will be given a deposit certificate).
Many third-party projects can freely use the market-making LP to distribute the benefits and the way of distribution for different purposes. It is determined by a third-party project, for marketers, it is just free income.



3. Step by step guide on how to do mining

Open your wallet, enter DeFis DAPP.
Find mining in the transaction, select the corresponding CDOG/EOS, CDOG/USDT trading pair to participate in mining.
